What this is

Eight named drivers from a 24-driver / 14-team roster line up on a procedural Catmull-Rom circuit — corners, hairpins, chicane, pit lane. Lights out, race begins. Grip-circle physics, drafting at +5%, DRS zones at +10%, tire wear that degrades grip per lap. Per-driver pit windows vary the strategy. Random safety car after a spin. Position table tracks gap, pit status, and tire bar. Twelve laps, then a podium ceremony with confetti, full classification, and the fastest lap highlighted.

Why this is mind-blowing

Real F1 storylines emerge. Test runs produced a 5-lead-change race won by 1.83 seconds, a DRS-train overtake from P4 to P1 in a single lap, magic laps on worn rubber, late-race safety cars that bunched the field. The 24-driver roster means each race has a different lineup of liveries to root for. Six minutes of motorsport drama, then a fresh circuit with a new lineup.
